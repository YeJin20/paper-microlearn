You are designing a 10-day microlearning curriculum to help an 
autonomous driving researcher deeply understand a single paper 
through brief, KakaoTalk-style daily messages.

## CONTEXT
- Reader: senior AI/autonomous driving researcher
- Currently exploring: scene editing for data augmentation
- Reading mode: mobile, 3-5 minutes per day
- Goal after Day 10: reader can explain the paper to a peer, 
  knows where it sits in the field, sees concrete application 
  angles for their own work

## DAY-BY-DAY RUBRIC (follow exactly)

Day 1 — Why this problem matters
  Motivation. Tie to sim2real, digital twin, data augmentation.
  DO NOT introduce the method yet.

Day 2 — Why prior work fell short
  Limitations of NeRF-based approaches (Block-NeRF, S-NeRF).

Day 3 — Core idea in one sentence
  (a) one-sentence summary  
  (b) one intuition/analogy  
  (c) why 3D Gaussians > NeRF for this problem

Day 4 — Dynamic/Static decoupling landscape
  Compare NSG, MARS, EmerNeRF, deformation-based 4D-GS. 
  Position Street Gaussians. Trade-off of each.

Day 5 — Method overview: the full rendering pipeline
  Big picture only. How background + vehicle Gaussians compose 
  at render time. Show the FLOW, not component details.

Day 6 — Background model
  Static Gaussians + semantic logits. Why semantic logits are 
  needed (sky regularization, scene understanding).

Day 7 — Vehicle dynamics: tracked-pose attachment
  μ_world = R_t · μ_local + t_t. Why per-vehicle local frames. 
  Pose as learnable variable — robustness to tracker noise.

Day 8 — 4D Spherical Harmonics
  Why standard SH fails for moving vehicles (rotation × lighting). 
  How time-Fourier basis multiplied with SH solves it.

Day 9 — Results that matter
  Key numbers ACTUALLY IN THE PAPER. What each enables for AD.

Day 10 — Scene editing for data augmentation
  How explicit + decoupled representation enables editing:
  add/remove/relocate vehicles, lighting via SH.
  Mention follow-ups (DrivingGaussian, OmniRe).
  End with concrete reflection question for reader's own work.

## CONSTRAINTS (apply to every day)
- Length: 400-600 Korean characters
- Tone: conversational, peer-to-peer
- Must include: one concrete example or analogy
- Must NOT: dry abstract; repeat across days; academic jargon 
  without explanation

## FACT GROUNDING (CRITICAL)
- Only use numbers explicitly stated in the paper.
- If uncertain, OMIT or hedge with "약/추정/대략".
- Do NOT invent benchmark scores or comparison numbers.

- suggested_figure: "Figure X — brief description" (or null)
