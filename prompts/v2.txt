You are designing a 10-day microlearning curriculum to help an 
autonomous driving researcher deeply understand a single paper 
through brief, KakaoTalk-style daily messages.

## CONTEXT
- Reader: senior AI/autonomous driving researcher
- Currently exploring: {goal}
- Reading mode: mobile, 3-5 minutes per day
- Goal after Day 10: reader can explain the paper to a peer, 
  knows where it sits in the field, sees concrete application 
  angles for their own work

## DAY-BY-DAY RUBRIC (follow exactly)

Day 1 — Why this problem matters
  Motivation. Tie to sim2real, digital twin, data augmentation.
  DO NOT introduce the method yet.

Day 2 — Why prior work fell short
  Limitations of NeRF-based approaches.

Day 3 — Core idea in one sentence
  (a) one-sentence summary  (b) one intuition/analogy  
  (c) why this approach > previous

Day 4 — Landscape (compare alternatives)
  Compare related approaches, position this paper, trade-offs.

Day 5 — Method overview: the full pipeline
  Big picture only. The "map" before the deep dive.

Day 6-8 — Method deep dives (3 days of component-by-component)
  One major component per day. Equations + plain-language intuition.

Day 9 — Results that matter
  Key numbers ACTUALLY IN THE PAPER. What each enables.

Day 10 — Application to reader's work
  Specific application angle: {goal}.
  Mention follow-up work.
  End with reflection question for reader's own research.

## CONSTRAINTS (apply to every day)
- Length: 400-600 Korean characters for the main content
- Tone: conversational, peer-to-peer
- Must include: one concrete example or analogy
- Must NOT: dry abstract; repeat across days; jargon without explanation
- Emphasis: Use Markdown **bold** for key technical terms, important 
  numbers, central concepts (e.g., **4D Spherical Harmonics**, 
  **135 FPS**). 2-4 emphases per day. Don't overuse.

## FACT GROUNDING (CRITICAL)
- Only use numbers explicitly in the paper.
- If uncertain, OMIT or hedge with "약/추정/대략".

## ADDITIONAL CONTENT (every day must have these)

### key_concepts (2-4 items)
Important technical terms from this day's content.
Each: term (original form, EN or KO), definition (1-2 plain sentences).

### english_expressions (2-3 items)
Useful English academic writing phrases that appear (or could appear) 
in this paper. Focus on phrases a non-native English researcher would 
want to learn for their own writing.
Each: expression (EN phrase), korean (short KO meaning), 
example (sentence using it, from or paraphrasing the paper), 
context (when this phrase is used in academic writing).

### mcq (exactly 2 questions)
Substantive multiple choice (not trivial recall) verifying understanding 
of this day's content.
Each: question, options (array of exactly 4 KR options), 
correct (0-3 index), explanation (why correct, brief).

### open_question (exactly 1)
A question an industry practitioner (AD researcher/engineer) might 
ask after this day. Should make the reader think + connect to own work.
- question: the question
- hint: nudges toward the right direction without giving away
- key_takeaway: the ONE insight reader should walk away with from 
  this question, regardless of their answer. Shown after they submit.

## FIGURE SUGGESTIONS
For each day, suggest ONE figure or table.
suggested_figure: "Figure X — brief description" or null.
figure_page: PDF page number (1-indexed) of that figure, or null.

## THOUGHT TRIGGER (vary across days)
Rotate among 3 types:
- A (next-day tease)
- B (self-check question)  
- C (try-this-mentally)
Each type at least 3 times across 10 days. Day 10 must be C.
